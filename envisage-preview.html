The Sapphire Engine

Built for Scale and Flexibility

Sapphire is written in Python, with a PostgreSQL back-end connecting to an Elasticsearch engine linked to major technologies and cloud storage services — Amazon, Azure, Caspio, and Google tools — standardized through Fivetran ETL to a Snowflake data warehouse.

Outputs are delivered into visualization applications including Tableau, Sentinel, GIS mapping apps, and more. The result: a system that links the largest data repositories to on-the-ground contextual intelligence in real time.

Legacy systems can be connected to field data for real-time monitoring and assessment, making Sapphire an integration layer as much as a platform.

The Value of Sapphire + DaaS

Strategic Consulting, Powered by Data

Sapphire combined with our Data as a Service model allows us to provide strategic consulting in a truly unique way. Data and AI-scraped, structured content is loaded into our proprietary system where clients can leverage curated knowledge sets for customized concept mapping, strategic planning, and due diligence analysis.

Strategic mind mapping and rapid understanding of complex issues is possible as our systems make quick sense of otherwise hard-to-recall facts.

One of our most significant innovations is the use of ontological logic within datasets — building a database engine that assembles person-relationship "WHO's" on any subject rapidly.

Leveraging Visualization

One of our organization's most significant ideas is the use of ontological logic within our datasets. This allows for data aggregation, searching, and retrieval of nearly unprecedented power and flexibility — building a database engine that assembles person-relationship "WHO's" on any subject rapidly.

The ENVISAGE engine serves as the basis for an algorithm with unique IP potential — a system that runs algorithms to search and self-organize data, looking for persons and key dimensions of information (education, politics, companies), taking unstructured data and making it structured, aggregating with ontologies and ranking and propensity formulas to create a person-to-person relationship compiler.

Origin Story

SAM is the culmination of years of experience and innovation by ENVISAGE, a front-line research organization founded in 2013. Originally focused on improving analysis in conflict-affected regions, ENVISAGE developed its core platform, Sapphire, to integrate advanced technology, rigorous methodology, and field data with large-scale data repositories.

Its work has long supported research efforts for the United Nations and a network of partner embassies working to create social and economic change.

As the global landscape shifted and economic factors gained prominence, ENVISAGE recognized that traditional development efforts were changing. In response, the organization adapted by providing economic stakeholders with deeper data and insights. With the help of experts from the fund management industry, SAM was created to meet the needs and workflows of investment teams — while leveraging ENVISAGE's expertise in research and technology.

FAQ

Common Questions

What challenges does SAM solve?

SAM addresses fragmented research, over-reliance on key individuals, technology gaps, and the difficulty of proving strategy performance to stakeholders. It turns research from a cost center into a demonstrable, quantifiable asset.

Is SAM a replacement for Capital IQ or FactSet?

No — SAM is complementary. It integrates alongside big data providers like Capital IQ, FactSet, and HOLT, adding the research management and decision-tracking layer those tools don't provide.

Who is SAM designed for?

Small-to-medium fund managers who believe technology and data need to be leveraged to improve their business, want to know what their firm makes decisions on, and are committed to systems thinking, bias reduction, and continuous learning.

Industry Context

Looking at Research Through the Fund Manager's Lens

In today's rapidly evolving financial landscape, fund managers worldwide are seeking innovative ways to enhance their research and decision-making processes.

The traditional approach — where fund managers were only as good as their partners' instincts on companies — has undergone a dramatic transformation. The evolution can be seen in four distinct phases.

Phase 1

The Instinct Era

Research driven by partner experience and gut feelings. Limited data access and subjective decision-making dominated the process.

Phase 2

The Data Revolution

Structured data and financial terminals introduced rigor, but teams still relied on fragmented, individually-held knowledge.

Phase 3

Scalable Engagement

Platforms emerged for sharing and tracking research — but still lacked the closed-loop performance feedback teams needed.

Phase 4

The AI-Augmented Era

AI tools now augment every stage of research. The firms that survive will be those who structure their data to take full advantage.

The Blended Approach

Art Meets Science

The future of fund management isn't purely quantitative or qualitative — it's the disciplined combination of both.

Factor-Based Analysis

Systematic scoring of specific factors that lead to investment decisions — internal, external, and valuation-based.

Weighted Methodologies

Understanding how different factors are weighed and varied to improve results across strategies, geographies, and team members.

Performance Monitoring

Real-time tracking of decision outcomes and their impact on overall fund performance — closing the loop between process and result.

Bias Identification

Recognizing and addressing inherent biases in staff thinking and decision-making processes before they affect performance.
